Roshan Digital Account: a complete 2026 guide for overseas Pakistanis

Everything an overseas Pakistani needs to know to open, fund and operate an Allied Bank Roshan Digital Account (RDA) in 2026. Eligibility, account types, the full online application, Naya Pakistan Certificates, Roshan Apna Ghar, Roshan Equity, taxation and frequently asked questions.

TL;DR

Three things to know

  • The Roshan Digital Account is the State Bank of Pakistan's official online account for overseas Pakistanis. You open it from anywhere, with no branch visit.
  • Inside one RDA umbrella you can hold up to four sub-accounts (NRP in rupees, FCY in your home currency, RFCA for repatriated funds, and an Investment Account) and access Naya Pakistan Certificates, Roshan Apna Ghar, Roshan Equity, and Roshan Pension.
  • Opening takes about 15 minutes online plus a one-day verification. Allied Bank emails your IBAN within one working day.

What is the Roshan Digital Account?

The Roshan Digital Account is a digital onboarding programme launched by the State Bank of Pakistan in September 2020 that lets overseas Pakistanis and Pakistan-origin individuals open a Pakistani bank account fully online, without ever visiting a branch. As of 2026 the programme has attracted more than 8 billion US dollars in cumulative inflows from over 175 countries, making it the single largest formal-channel diaspora banking initiative in South Asia.

RDA was created to do two things at the same time. The first goal was to give the diaspora a clean, regulated route back into the Pakistani financial system. The second was to redirect informal remittance flows (hawala, hundi, money mules) into the banking channel where State Bank rules apply and anti-money-laundering checks happen automatically.

Allied Bank was an early implementing bank in the RDA programme and offers the complete Roshan Digital Services product family today: the core RDA, a Roshan Digital Business Account for diaspora-owned SMEs, a low-income RDA variant, Naya Pakistan Certificate subscription, Roshan Apna Ghar home financing, Allied Roshan Apni Car, Roshan Equity Investment, Roshan Pension Plan and Roshan Samaaji Khidmat for community giving.

Who can open a Roshan Digital Account?

You are eligible for an Allied Bank RDA if you hold a valid Pakistani CNIC, NICOP or POC, regardless of which country you currently live in. The State Bank's regulatory framework recognises three eligible groups: non-resident Pakistani nationals, Pakistan-origin foreign nationals holding a Pakistan Origin Card (POC), and resident Pakistanis with declared foreign-source income.

The eligibility test is straightforward in practice. If you have any one of the three identity documents above and are physically resident outside Pakistan when you apply, you qualify. Pakistan-origin individuals (former nationals who have taken citizenship abroad) qualify even without a current Pakistani passport, using their POC.

Documents you will need

  • A clear scan of your CNIC or NICOP (both sides), or your POC plus a foreign passport.
  • A recent passport-size photograph.
  • A foreign address proof (utility bill, bank statement or tenancy agreement) dated within the last three months.
  • Proof of source of funds (employment letter, salary slip, business registration or tax return) for amounts above State Bank thresholds.

Allied Bank currently accepts RDA applications from more than 175 countries. If you live in a jurisdiction that is on the Financial Action Task Force grey or black list, expect additional documentation requirements and a longer verification window.

How to open your RDA with Allied Bank

Opening an Allied Roshan Digital Account takes about 15 minutes online plus a one-day verification window. You upload your CNIC or NICOP, a passport scan, recent proof of address, and complete a short live video KYC. Once approved, you receive your IBAN by email and SMS within one working day.

  1. Start the application. Visit the Allied Roshan Digital Account page and click Open Account. You will be asked to choose between an individual RDA, a joint RDA, or a Roshan Digital Business Account.
  2. Upload your documents. CNIC or NICOP scan, passport, foreign address proof, photograph. The portal accepts JPG, PNG and PDF up to 5 MB per file.
  3. Complete the digital KYC. A 60-second live video call with an Allied Bank representative verifies your identity. Pick a slot any working day between 09:00 and 21:00 Pakistan time.
  4. Receive your IBAN. Your IBAN, debit card details and myABL credentials arrive by email and SMS within one working day. You are ready to fund and operate the account.

The four RDA account types and what they hold

The Allied Roshan Digital Account opens as a single relationship containing up to four sub-accounts: a PKR-denominated Non-Resident Pakistani Rupee account, a Foreign Currency Account (USD, GBP, EUR, AED or SAR), a Resident Foreign Currency Account for funds repatriated into Pakistan through the banking channel, and an Investment Account for Naya Pakistan Certificates and Roshan Equity. Each carries different repatriability and tax characteristics under State Bank rules.

Sub-accountCurrencyBest forRepatriable
NRP (Non-Resident Pakistani Rupee)PKRDay-to-day spending and family transfers in PakistanYes, in PKR
FCY (Foreign Currency)USD, GBP, EUR, AED, SARHolding foreign currency without converting to PKRYes, in original currency
RFCA (Resident Foreign Currency Account)USD, GBP, EURRepatriated funds that you may bring back homeYes, with documentation

Most overseas Pakistanis start with one NRP and one FCY sub-account. RFCA is typically added later when funds are brought back into Pakistan through a separate banking channel. The Investment Account is opened when you first subscribe to a Naya Pakistan Certificate or place an equity order.

What you can do with your RDA

Once your RDA is active you can hold, send and convert funds, invest in Naya Pakistan Certificates, finance a home in Pakistan via Allied Roshan Apna Ghar, finance a vehicle via Allied Roshan Apni Car, invest in the Pakistan Stock Exchange via Roshan Equity Investment, build retirement savings via Roshan Pension Plan, and donate to vetted causes via Roshan Samaaji Khidmat. Every product is operated entirely online from your home country.

Investment

Naya Pakistan Certificates are sovereign instruments issued by the Government of Pakistan exclusively to RDA holders, in tenors from three months to five years, in PKR or USD, with periodic profit pay-outs and a final 10 percent withholding tax. Roshan Equity Investment lets you place orders on the Pakistan Stock Exchange directly from your RDA, in PKR, through Allied Bank's brokerage partners.

Financing

Allied Roshan Apna Ghar is a home-financing programme for overseas Pakistanis built on the Diminishing Musharakah structure. Allied Roshan Apni Car provides car financing on the Aitebar Car Ijarah lease structure. Both are Shariah-compliant and disbursed directly to dealers and developers in Pakistan.

Retirement and giving

Roshan Pension Plan is a State Bank-regulated voluntary retirement programme for non-resident Pakistanis with tax-advantaged contributions. Roshan Samaaji Khidmat is the donation gateway for vetted Pakistani charities and Allied-curated relief funds.

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU

If you are a Pakistani earning abroad, your RDA is the bridge.

One online relationship gives you access to every regulated route money can take between your home country and Pakistan: spending, sending, saving, investing in NPCs or PSX, financing a house or a car, and building a pension. Almost everything that previously required a branch visit is now done from your phone.

Funding your RDA from abroad

You fund your Allied RDA via SWIFT wire from any internationally regulated bank, via licensed money transfer operators such as Wise, Remitly, MoneyGram and Western Union, or by direct deposit at Allied Bank correspondent partners in the Gulf, the United Kingdom and North America. Transfers settle within one to three working days; SWIFT typically clears same-day if your originating bank cuts off before 14:00 local time.

Allied Bank's IBAN appears on your welcome email and inside the myABL app. Most overseas Pakistanis use SWIFT for one-time large transfers (home down-payments, NPC investments) and a money-transfer operator for routine family support. Money-transfer operators usually clear within minutes at a higher per-transaction cost; SWIFT is cheaper per dollar but slower.

Funds arriving in your FCY sub-account stay in the original currency. Funds arriving in NRP are auto-converted at the prevailing Allied Bank inter-bank rate, with the conversion shown on your statement. There is no incoming transfer fee at the Allied end; the originating bank or operator may apply its own.

Tax and regulatory considerations

RDA balances and earnings receive a preferential withholding-tax treatment under the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001 as amended for diaspora investment. Pakistani tax does not apply to principal capital and a 10 percent final tax applies to profit on Naya Pakistan Certificates and Roshan Equity dividends. Your home-country tax obligations are independent and must be filed under your local rules. Check the country-specific guides linked below before transferring large sums.

The 10 percent final tax means the profit is taxed once, at source, and you do not need to refile under Pakistan's normal Active Taxpayer regime. This is one of the structural advantages that makes NPC competitive with comparable diaspora bonds elsewhere.

You retain full repatriability. Funds that came in as foreign currency can leave as foreign currency, in the same channel, at any time. State Bank anti-money-laundering checks run automatically on outflows above prescribed thresholds; expect a 24 to 72-hour additional verification window on large repatriations.

For US taxpayers, the IRS treats the RDA as a foreign financial account; you may need to file FBAR (FinCEN Form 114) and FATCA Form 8938 depending on aggregate balances. For UK taxpayers, declare RDA-generated foreign income on the SA106 supplement of your Self Assessment return. For UAE residents, no personal income tax applies in 2026, but check Allied's UAE country guide for documentation expected during AML review.

Operating your RDA day to day

You operate your Allied RDA entirely through the Roshan Digital Portal and the myABL mobile and web apps, with the same biometric, OTP and device-binding controls used by resident Allied customers. You can pay bills in Pakistan, send PKR transfers via IBFT, top up family debit cards, manage your card limits, and download monthly statements without contacting a branch.

Most account-management actions take place inside myABL. The Roshan Digital Portal is used for actions specific to non-residents: initiating a Naya Pakistan Certificate purchase, placing a Roshan Equity order, or beginning a Roshan Apna Ghar application. Both share the same login credentials.

If you change phone number or device, you can re-bind biometric authentication in 60 seconds without losing access. If you forget your password, the recovery flow uses your CNIC, OTP and a video step. Customer support is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week through chat inside the app and through a dedicated overseas Pakistanis helpline.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to open an Allied Roshan Digital Account?

The online application takes about 15 minutes. After you complete the live video KYC, Allied Bank typically activates your account and emails your IBAN within one working day.

Who is eligible for an RDA with Allied Bank?

Any non-resident Pakistani national with a valid CNIC or NICOP, Pakistan-origin foreign nationals holding a POC, and resident Pakistanis with declared foreign-source income are eligible to open a Roshan Digital Account under State Bank of Pakistan regulations.

What is the difference between NRP, RFCA, and FCY accounts inside an RDA?

NRP holds Pakistani Rupees, FCY holds your home currency (USD, GBP, EUR, AED, SAR), and RFCA holds foreign currency repatriated into Pakistan through the banking channel. Each has different repatriability and tax treatment under State Bank rules. See the table above for a side-by-side view.

Can I invest in Naya Pakistan Certificates through my RDA?

Yes. Once your Roshan Digital Account is active, you can subscribe to Naya Pakistan Certificates directly from your account in PKR or USD, with tenors ranging from three months to five years. Profit is paid out periodically and taxed at a final 10 percent under the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001.

Does Allied Bank charge fees for RDA account maintenance?

Account maintenance is governed by the Allied Bank Schedule of Charges in force at the time of the transaction. Most RDA-specific transactions, including SWIFT inward remittance and PKR transfers, are subject to State Bank-approved tariffs. Check the live Schedule of Charges before transferring large sums.

Can I open an RDA jointly with my spouse?

Yes. Joint Roshan Digital Accounts are supported when both account holders qualify under the RDA eligibility criteria. Both holders complete the digital KYC separately during onboarding.

Can I close my RDA and repatriate funds?

Yes. RDA principal balances and accrued returns are freely repatriable to your foreign bank account through the same banking channel used to fund the account, subject to verification by Allied Bank and the State Bank's anti-money-laundering checks.
