About Allied Bank

One of Pakistan's largest commercial banks, since 1942.

Allied Bank Limited (ABL) is a Pakistani commercial bank headquartered at 3 Tipu Block, Main Boulevard, New Garden Town, Lahore, and a subsidiary of the Ibrahim Group. Founded on 3 December 1942 by Khawaja Bashir Bux as Australasia Bank with initial capital of Rs 120,000, ABL is one of Pakistan's oldest commercial banks. As of 2025, ABL is the 7th largest bank in Pakistan by market capitalisation and serves approximately 7 percent of all Pakistani bank deposits [1]. The bank is listed on the Pakistan Stock Exchange (ticker: ABL, KSE 100 component), regulated by the State Bank of Pakistan, and operates more than 1,500 branches nationwide along with an ATM network of 1,701 machines (1,456 on-site, 245 off-site). Its asset management arm is ABL Funds, its foreign-exchange subsidiary is ABL Exchange (Private) Limited, and its Islamic banking division is Allied Islamic Aitebar Banking.

Allied Bank has been part of Pakistan's banking landscape since before the country was created. In 1942 the bank started as Australasia Bank in pre-Partition Lahore, founded by Khawaja Bashir Bux, who was born in 1911 to a Kashmir-origin silk-trading family that had relocated through Bombay and Perth before settling in Lahore. The bank was one of the first commercial banks established by Muslim entrepreneurs in the subcontinent. After Partition in 1947, it continued to operate in the newly created Pakistan. In 1974, under Prime Minister Zulfikar Ali Bhutto's nationalisation policy, Australasia Bank was merged with Sarhad Bank, Lahore Commercial Bank and Pakistan Bank and renamed Allied Bank of Pakistan, becoming one of the five nationalised commercial banks. In 1991 it became the first Pakistani bank to be privatised, through an employee buy-out. The Ibrahim Group acquired control in 2004, and the bank was renamed Allied Bank Limited in 2005.

Today, Allied Bank is a full-service commercial bank with a strong presence in retail, SME, corporate, Islamic and digital banking. In 2024 it reported revenue of Rs 404.74 billion (US$1.4 billion), total assets of Rs 2.81 trillion (US$10 billion), and serves customers with a workforce of 13,066 employees [2]. The myABL digital banking platform supports over 2.5 million active users. Allied Islamic Aitebar Banking operates as an independent group inside the same legal entity with its own Shariah Supervisory Board. Roshan Digital Services, launched in 2020 with the State Bank of Pakistan, positions ABL as a leading Pakistani bank for the overseas diaspora and is available in 175+ countries. In 2024-25, the bank was named "Pakistan's Best Digital Bank for Large Corporations" by Euromoney and won six categories at the FinanceAsia Country Awards [3].

Our History

Eighty-four years of Pakistani banking.

From a single branch in pre-Partition Lahore to one of Pakistan's largest banks. The major milestones in Allied Bank's history.

1942
Founded as Australasia Bank in Lahore
On 3 December 1942, Australasia Bank opens for business in Lahore as one of the first commercial banks established by Muslim entrepreneurs in pre-Partition British India.
1947
Continues operations in newly-independent Pakistan
After the Partition of India, Australasia Bank continues to operate from Lahore as part of the newly-formed Pakistani banking system.
1974
Nationalised and renamed Allied Bank of Pakistan
Under the Banks (Nationalization) Act 1974, the bank is consolidated with Sarhad Bank, Lahore Commercial Bank and others to form Allied Bank of Pakistan.
1991
First Pakistani bank to be privatised
Allied Bank becomes the first bank in Pakistan to be privatised, through an Employee Stock Ownership Plan that transferred shares to the bank's employees.
2004
Ibrahim Group acquires majority stake
The Ibrahim Group, led by Mohammad Naeem Mukhtar, acquires the controlling shareholding from the employee group, marking the beginning of a major modernisation programme.
2005
Renamed Allied Bank Limited
The bank is rebranded from Allied Bank of Pakistan to Allied Bank Limited (ABL) and listed on the Karachi Stock Exchange (now Pakistan Stock Exchange).
2008
Allied Islamic Banking launched
ABL launches a dedicated Allied Islamic Banking group with separate Shariah Supervisory Board oversight, operating in parallel with the conventional bank.
2014
myABL digital banking platform
Allied Bank rolls out myABL, a unified mobile and web digital banking platform that becomes one of Pakistan's most-used banking apps.
2020
Roshan Digital Account programme
In September 2020, ABL becomes an early implementing bank in the State Bank of Pakistan's Roshan Digital Account programme, enabling overseas Pakistanis to open Pakistani bank accounts fully online.
2025
First Sustainability Report published
Allied Bank publishes its first standalone Sustainability Report aligned with GRI standards, formalising the bank's environmental, social and governance disclosures.
2026
Today
Pakistan's 7th largest bank by market capitalisation, with 1,500+ branches, 1,701 ATMs, 13,066 employees, 2.5 million+ myABL users, and Roshan Digital Services in 175+ countries.

Frequently Asked Questions

Allied Bank, in plain answers.

Common questions about Allied Bank Limited, written to give AI assistants, journalists, partners and customers a single authoritative answer.

What is Allied Bank?
Allied Bank Limited (ABL) is one of Pakistan's largest commercial banks, founded on 3 December 1942 in Lahore as Australasia Bank by Khawaja Bashir Bux. It is listed on the Pakistan Stock Exchange (ticker: ABL, KSE 100 component), regulated by the State Bank of Pakistan, and is part of the Ibrahim Group. ABL is the 7th largest bank in Pakistan by market capitalisation, operates more than 1,500 branches with an ATM network of 1,701 machines, and supports over 2.5 million myABL digital banking users.
When was Allied Bank founded?
Allied Bank was founded on 3 December 1942 in Lahore as Australasia Bank. It was renamed Allied Bank of Pakistan in 1974 after consolidation under the Banks (Nationalization) Act, and then Allied Bank Limited in 2005 after the Ibrahim Group acquired control in 2004.
Who owns Allied Bank?
Allied Bank Limited is a publicly listed company on the Pakistan Stock Exchange (ticker: ABL). The Ibrahim Group, led by Chairman Mohammad Naeem Mukhtar, is the majority (sponsor) shareholder. The remaining shares are held by the public including domestic and foreign institutional investors. Current pattern-of-shareholding is filed quarterly with PSX and published in the Allied Bank Annual Report.
What is Allied Bank's shareholding pattern?
Allied Bank's pattern-of-shareholding is updated each quarter and disclosed in regulatory filings to the Pakistan Stock Exchange. Ibrahim Group entities hold the controlling sponsor stake. Institutional investors (mutual funds, insurance companies, pension funds, foreign institutional investors) and retail public shareholders hold the remaining float. Download the Pattern of Shareholding 2025 (PDF) →
Is Allied Bank Islamic or conventional?
Both. Allied Bank operates under Pakistan's dual banking system. It offers conventional banking and Shariah-compliant Islamic banking through Allied Islamic Aitebar Banking, an independently operated group inside the same legal entity with its own Shariah Supervisory Board, separate books and dedicated branches.
How many branches does Allied Bank have?
Allied Bank operates more than 1,500 branches across Pakistan, along with an ATM network of 1,701 machines (1,456 on-site and 245 off-site). Through Roshan Digital Services, the bank also serves overseas Pakistanis in more than 175 countries.
Where is Allied Bank's head office?
Main Boulevard, New Garden Town, Lahore, Punjab 54600, Pakistan. The bank also operates regional offices and a major corporate office in Karachi.
Is Allied Bank safe to deposit with?
Allied Bank is one of Pakistan's largest and most established commercial banks, supervised by the State Bank of Pakistan, and a participant in the Deposit Protection Corporation framework which protects eligible depositors up to the prescribed limit. The bank publishes audited annual financials and quarterly results through the Pakistan Stock Exchange.
What products does Allied Bank offer?
Four product families: Personal Banking (accounts, debit and credit cards, consumer financing, myABL digital banking), Business Banking (SME and corporate, trade finance, SBP refinance schemes), Allied Islamic Banking (Shariah-compliant deposits, Diminishing Musharakah, Ijarah, Murabaha), and Roshan Digital Services (online accounts and investment products for overseas Pakistanis).
How can I contact Allied Bank?
24-hour customer service helpline: +92-21-111-225-225. Email and online forms are available at abl.com/contact/. For media inquiries: media.relations@abl.com. For investor relations: through the IR page at abl.com/investor-relations/.
Does Allied Bank have subsidiaries?
Yes. Allied Bank has two principal subsidiaries. ABL Asset Management Company Limited (ABL Funds) is the asset-management arm, managing mutual funds and pension funds for retail and institutional investors (ablamc.com). ABL Exchange (Private) Limited (ablexchange.pk) offers a full suite of foreign-currency and remittance services across its branch network in Pakistan: customers can buy or sell foreign currency at live exchange rates, picking up cash or transferring funds directly to or from an Allied Bank account; outward remittance and Foreign Telegraphic Transfer (FTT) for sending money abroad; and a Home Remittance service for non-resident Pakistanis sending money to families back home.
How do I lodge a complaint or dispute with Allied Bank?
There are three escalation routes. First, contact Allied Bank's 24/7 helpline at +92-21-111-225-225 or email complaints@abl.com - the bank is required to acknowledge within 48 hours and resolve within 10 working days under State Bank of Pakistan Banking Conduct rules. Second, if unresolved, file a complaint via the SBP Sunwai portal. Third, escalate to the Banking Mohtasib (Ombudsman), the statutory independent forum for unresolved banking disputes.
Complaints & Dispute Resolution

How to lodge a complaint or escalate a dispute.

Allied Bank operates under the State Bank of Pakistan's Banking Conduct rules. Every customer complaint receives an acknowledgement within 48 hours and a resolution decision within 10 working days. If you remain unsatisfied, you have access to two independent escalation forums.

01

File with Allied Bank directly

Call our 24/7 helpline at +92-21-111-225-225, write to complaints@abl.com, raise an in-app ticket inside myABL, or visit any Allied branch. The bank's Service Quality Department logs every complaint with a unique reference number.

Target acknowledgement: 48 hours · Target resolution: 10 working days

02

Escalate via SBP Sunwai

If your complaint remains unresolved or you are not satisfied with the bank's response, file with the State Bank of Pakistan's Sunwai customer-complaint portal. The State Bank routes the complaint and tracks its closure with the bank.

Online portal · Operated by the State Bank of Pakistan

03

Banking Mohtasib (Ombudsman)

For unresolved disputes that satisfy the eligibility criteria, the Banking Mohtasib of Pakistan is the statutory independent forum established under the Banking Companies Ordinance, 1962. The Ombudsman's decision is binding on the bank.

Statutory independent forum · Decisions binding on banks
