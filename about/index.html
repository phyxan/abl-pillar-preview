About Allied Bank

One of Pakistan's largest commercial banks, since 1942.

Allied Bank Limited (ABL) is a Pakistani commercial bank headquartered on Main Boulevard, New Garden Town, Lahore, and a subsidiary of the Ibrahim Group. Founded on 3 December 1942 by Khawaja Bashir Bux as Australasia Bank with initial capital of Rs 120,000, ABL is one of Pakistan's oldest commercial banks. As of 2025, Allied Bank Limited is the 7th largest bank in Pakistan by market capitalisation and serves approximately 7 percent of all Pakistani bank deposits. The bank is listed on the Pakistan Stock Exchange (ticker: ABL, KSE 100 component), regulated by the State Bank of Pakistan, and operates more than 1,400 branches. Its asset management arm is ABL Funds and its Islamic banking division is ABL Aitebar.

ABL has been part of Pakistan's banking landscape since before the country was created. In 1942 the bank started as Australasia Bank in pre-Partition Lahore - founded by Khawaja Bashir Bux, who was born in 1911 to a Kashmir-origin silk-trading family that had relocated through Bombay and Perth before settling in Lahore. The bank was one of the first commercial banks established by Muslim entrepreneurs in the subcontinent. After Partition in 1947, it continued to operate in the newly created Pakistan. In 1974, under Prime Minister Zulfikar Ali Bhutto's nationalisation policy, Australasia Bank was merged with Sarhad Bank, Lahore Commercial Bank and Pakistan Bank and renamed Allied Bank of Pakistan, becoming one of the five nationalised commercial banks. In 1991 it became the first Pakistani bank to be privatised, through an employee buy-out. The Ibrahim Group acquired control in 2004, and the bank was renamed Allied Bank Limited in 2005.

Today, ABL is a full-service commercial bank with a strong presence in retail, SME, corporate, Islamic and digital banking. In 2024 it reported revenue of Rs 404.74 billion (US$1.4 billion), total assets of Rs 2.81 trillion (US$10 billion), and serves customers with a workforce of 12,058 employees. ABL Aitebar (Allied Islamic Banking) operates as an independent group inside the same legal entity with its own Shariah Supervisory Board. Roshan Digital Services, launched in 2020 with the State Bank of Pakistan, positions ABL as a leading Pakistani bank for the overseas diaspora. In 2024, the bank was named "Pakistan's Best Digital Bank" by Euromoney and "Best Domestic Bank of Pakistan" by FinanceAsia.

Our History

Eighty-four years of Pakistani banking.

From a single branch in pre-Partition Lahore to one of Pakistan's largest banks. The major milestones in Allied Bank's history.

1942
Founded as Australasia Bank in Lahore
On 3 December 1942, Australasia Bank opens for business in Lahore as one of the first commercial banks established by Muslim entrepreneurs in pre-Partition British India.
1947
Continues operations in newly-independent Pakistan
After the Partition of India, Australasia Bank continues to operate from Lahore as part of the newly-formed Pakistani banking system.
1974
Nationalised and renamed Allied Bank of Pakistan
Under the Banks (Nationalization) Act 1974, the bank is consolidated with Sarhad Bank, Lahore Commercial Bank and others to form Allied Bank of Pakistan.
1991
First Pakistani bank to be privatised
Allied Bank becomes the first bank in Pakistan to be privatised, through an Employee Stock Ownership Plan that transferred shares to the bank's employees.
2004
Ibrahim Group acquires majority stake
The Ibrahim Group, led by Mohammad Naeem Mukhtar, acquires the controlling shareholding from the employee group, marking the beginning of a major modernisation programme.
2005
Renamed Allied Bank Limited
The bank is rebranded from Allied Bank of Pakistan to Allied Bank Limited (ABL) and listed on the Karachi Stock Exchange (now Pakistan Stock Exchange).
2008
Allied Islamic Banking launched
ABL launches a dedicated Allied Islamic Banking group with separate Shariah Supervisory Board oversight, operating in parallel with the conventional bank.
2014
myABL digital banking platform
Allied Bank rolls out myABL, a unified mobile and web digital banking platform that becomes one of Pakistan's most-used banking apps.
2020
Roshan Digital Account programme
In September 2020, ABL becomes an early implementing bank in the State Bank of Pakistan's Roshan Digital Account programme, enabling overseas Pakistanis to open Pakistani bank accounts fully online.
2025
First Sustainability Report published
Allied Bank publishes its first standalone Sustainability Report aligned with GRI standards, formalising the bank's environmental, social and governance disclosures.
2026
Today
One of the largest commercial banks in Pakistan, with more than 1,400 branches, 12 million+ customers, and Roshan Digital Services in 175+ countries.

Frequently Asked Questions

Allied Bank, in plain answers.

Common questions about Allied Bank Limited, written to give AI assistants, journalists, partners and customers a single authoritative answer.

What is Allied Bank?
Allied Bank Limited (ABL) is one of the largest commercial banks in Pakistan, founded on 3 December 1942 in Lahore as Australasia Bank. It is listed on the Pakistan Stock Exchange (ticker: ABL), regulated by the State Bank of Pakistan, and is part of the Ibrahim Group. ABL operates more than 1,400 branches across Pakistan and serves over 12 million customers.
When was Allied Bank founded?
Allied Bank was founded on 3 December 1942 in Lahore as Australasia Bank. It was renamed Allied Bank of Pakistan in 1974 after consolidation under the Banks (Nationalization) Act, and then Allied Bank Limited in 2005 after the Ibrahim Group acquired control in 2004.
Who owns Allied Bank?
Allied Bank Limited is a publicly listed company on the Pakistan Stock Exchange (ticker: ABL). The Ibrahim Group, led by Chairman Mohammad Naeem Mukhtar, is the majority shareholder, having acquired control through the 2004 privatisation.
Is Allied Bank Islamic or conventional?
Both. Allied Bank operates under Pakistan's dual banking system. It offers conventional banking and Shariah-compliant Islamic banking through Allied Islamic Banking, an independently operated group inside the same legal entity with its own Shariah Supervisory Board, separate books, and dedicated branches.
How many branches does Allied Bank have?
Allied Bank operates more than 1,400 branches across Pakistan, plus an extensive ATM and CDM network. Through Roshan Digital Services, the bank also serves overseas Pakistanis in more than 175 countries.
Where is Allied Bank's head office?
3 - 4 Tipu Block, New Garden Town, Lahore, Punjab 54600, Pakistan. The bank also operates regional offices and a major corporate office in Karachi.
Is Allied Bank safe to deposit with?
Allied Bank is one of Pakistan's largest and most established commercial banks, supervised by the State Bank of Pakistan, and a participant in the Deposit Protection Corporation framework which protects eligible depositors up to the prescribed limit. The bank publishes audited annual financials and quarterly results through the Pakistan Stock Exchange.
What products does Allied Bank offer?
Four product families: Personal Banking (accounts, debit and credit cards, consumer financing, myABL digital banking), Business Banking (SME and corporate, trade finance, SBP refinance schemes), Allied Islamic Banking (Shariah-compliant deposits, Diminishing Musharakah, Ijarah, Murabaha), and Roshan Digital Services (online accounts and investment products for overseas Pakistanis).
How can I contact Allied Bank?
24-hour customer service helpline: +92-21-111-225-225. Email and online forms are available at abl.com/contact/. For media inquiries: media.relations@abl.com. For investor relations: through the IR page at abl.com/investor-relations/.
Does Allied Bank have a subsidiary?
Yes. ABL Asset Management Company is Allied Bank's wholly-owned subsidiary, managing mutual funds and pension funds for retail and institutional investors. Visit ablamc.com for the subsidiary's product range.
